# Fibonacco Landing Page Playbook v2
## Content Templates, Persuasion Frameworks & AM Voice for All Campaign Types

> **Version:** 2.0 — May 2026  
> **Core principle:** The Account Manager is a customer advocate and project manager — never a salesperson. The SMB owner is the strategic decision-maker who spends their own money. Every page respects their time, speaks plainly, and makes the opportunity clear enough to act on NOW.

---

## WHAT WE ARE — THE PITCH IN 30 SECONDS

Before any framework or template, every person on the team needs to be able to say this:

**"We are [Community Name]'s newspaper, event calendar, downtown guide, local web network, and community media platform. We publish local news, promote local events, list local businesses, and connect residents to the businesses that serve them. No one else does this for [Community Name]. Not the local paper (if there still is one). Not Facebook. Not Google. Not Yelp. Us."**

**"There are 37 positions available for businesses who want to be part of this. [X] are already taken. When your category fills, you're on a waitlist. We're building this now and we want you in it."**

That's the foundation. Every landing page, every email, every meeting is a version of that. If a page can't trace back to those two paragraphs, it doesn't belong in the catalog.

## THE AM VOICE

The Account Manager (Sarah, Marcus, Emma) is NOT a salesperson. They are a customer advocate who manages the process of getting the business positioned in their community. Their voice is:

### What the AM sounds like:

- "I'm looking at [Community Name] right now and your category has [X] slots left. I want to make sure we get you in before they fill."
- "I put together your proposal. Take a look and let me know what questions you have so we can move forward."
- "I noticed you haven't had a chance to review your listing yet. I'm going to hold your position for another week, but I can't guarantee availability after that."
- "Here's your status update: your listing is live, your first article published Tuesday, and you've had 847 views this week. Next step is locking in your annual position."

### What the AM does NOT sound like:

- ~~"Imagine what your business could look like..."~~
- ~~"In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape..."~~
- ~~"Are you ready to take your business to the next level?"~~
- ~~"Let me show you the power of community marketing..."~~
- ~~"We'd love to partner with you on your growth journey..."~~

### AM operating principles:

1. **Status updates, not pitches.** "Here's where things stand" not "here's why you should buy."
2. **Action items, not suggestions.** "I need you to review this by Friday" not "whenever you get a chance."
3. **Risk flags, not fear.** "Your category has 2 slots left and I can't hold yours past [date]" not "you'll miss out on this incredible opportunity."
4. **Advocacy, not neutrality.** "I think you should lock this in. Here's why." The AM has an opinion and shares it — because that's what a good project manager does.
5. **Respect their time.** Every page should be consumable in under 5 minutes. If it takes longer, it's too long.
6. **Respect their intelligence.** They run a business. They understand money, competition, and timing. Don't explain concepts they already know.
7. **Respect their authority.** They make the decisions. They spend the money. The content should make them feel like the smart operator who saw this first — not a student being educated.

## THE OWNER PERSONA

Every landing page is written FOR this person:

**Who they are:** Owner or manager of a local business. They make decisions. They spend their own money (or control the budget). They're busy. They're practical. They don't have time for theory.

**What motivates them:**
- Being seen as the leader in their category/community
- Making smart business decisions before their competitors do
- Getting concrete results they can see and measure
- Not wasting money on things that don't work
- Being the person in their business who "gets it"

**What they need to hear:**
- What IS this (not what could it be)
- What do I GET (not what's the vision)
- What does it COST (not what's the value proposition)
- Why NOW (not why eventually)
- Who else is doing this (not theoretical benefits)

**What turns them off:**
- Being talked down to
- Marketing jargon
- Vague promises
- Long presentations when a page would do
- Feeling like they're being "sold"
- Being asked to "imagine" anything — show them

## LANGUAGE RULES (ALL TYPES)

### Banned words and phrases:
- "AI," "machine learning," "algorithm," "automated," "bot" — describe outcomes only
- "Platform," "ecosystem," "solution" — use specific names: Day.News, GoEventCity, Downtown Guide
- "Subscription" in early content — say "position," "membership," or "slot"
- "Imagine," "envision," "picture this" — show, don't imagine
- "Partner with us," "growth journey," "take your business to the next level"
- "In today's [anything]..."
- "Digital landscape," "ever-changing," "unprecedented"
- Any sentence that starts with "We believe..."

### Required framing:
- **We are the newspaper** — not "a content platform"
- **We are the event calendar** — not "an event discovery tool"
- **We are the downtown guide** — not "a business directory"
- **We are the local web** — not "a digital presence network"
- **Nobody else does this** — say it plainly, say it often
- **Slots are limited and real** — not manufactured scarcity
- **Your competitor is looking at this** — if true, say it

### Tone calibration:
- Write like a sharp colleague, not a marketer
- Shorter sentences. Fewer adjectives.
- If a sentence works without an adverb, kill the adverb
- Numbers over adjectives: "847 views" not "significant traffic"
- Specifics over generalities: "2 of 4 restaurant slots taken" not "limited availability"

## TYPE 1: HOOK

### Framework: AIDA — compressed to a single screen

**What it is:** A free, immediate action. Claim a listing. Post an event. Create a coupon. No commitment. No sign-up. Just do the thing.

**Why it works:** The best way to sell someone on the platform is to get them USING the platform. Once their listing is live, once their event is posted, once their coupon is out there — they're invested. The Hook creates investment without asking for money.

### Page Structure (single screen, no scrolling if possible)

```
HEADLINE: What they get — verb-first, specific
  "Your Business Listing is Ready — Claim It"
  "Post Your Next Event — Free, Takes 2 Minutes"
  "Create a Coupon — We'll Distribute It to [X] Local Readers"

WHAT THIS IS (2 sentences max):
  "[Community Name] Day.News publishes a business directory for every business
   in the community. Your listing already exists based on public information.
   Claim it to verify and enhance it."

THE THING (visual preview):
  Show them the listing / event / coupon as it will appear
  If personalized (Tier 3): use their actual business data
  If generic (Tier 1): use a compelling example from their category

PRIMARY CTA: "[Claim Your Listing]" / "[Post This Event]" / "[Create My Coupon]"
  Big button. Above the fold. No ambiguity about what happens when they click.

SECONDARY CTA: "[I Want to Customize First]" / "[See How It Works]"
  For the person who needs one more step before acting.

SLOT STATUS (bottom of page, always present):
  "[X] of 37 business positions filled in [Community Name]"
  "[Y] [Category] slots remaining"
  Small, factual, not highlighted. It's context, not pressure.
```

### What makes a good Hook vs. a bad one:

**Good Hooks:** Free, immediate, tangible, completable in under 2 minutes
- Claim Your Listing ✓
- Post Your Event ✓
- Create a Coupon ✓
- Post a Classified ✓
- Get Featured (submit for article) ✓

**Bad Hooks (reclassify these):**
- HOOK-009 "Become a Sponsor" → This is a $500+/month commitment = Conversion
- HOOK-012 "Influencer Program" → This IS the subscription product = Conversion
- HOOK-006 "CRM Integration" → This is setup/onboarding = How-To
- HOOK-007 "Featured Listing Upgrade" → This is an upsell = Conversion

## TYPE 2: EDUCATION

### Framework: Briefing — not lecture, not story, not lesson

**What it is:** A brief, direct explanation of what Fibonacco is, what a specific capability does, or why a specific aspect of local marketing matters RIGHT NOW. Written for a busy owner who will give you 3-4 minutes.

**What it is NOT:**
- Not a course
- Not a webinar
- Not a thought leadership piece
- Not "AI Marketing 101" — nobody cares about 101 anything

**The reframe:** Every Education campaign should answer one of these questions from the owner's perspective:
- "What is this and why should I care?" (about Fibonacco or a feature)
- "What am I missing that my competitors aren't?" (competitive framing)
- "How does this affect my business right now?" (relevance)

### Page Structure

```
HEADLINE: Direct question or statement — from the owner's perspective
  "Your Customers Are Finding Your Competitors. Here's How."
  "What [Community Name] Day.News Does for Local Businesses"
  "The 3 Things That Determine Whether Locals Find You or Skip You"

THE SITUATION (3-4 sentences — what's happening):
  Not theory. Not trends. What is literally happening right now.
  "When someone in [Community] searches for [category], here's what they see: [list].
   If you're not in those results, you don't exist to that customer.
   Here's what determines whether you show up."

  Or: "There are 5 places people in [Community] go to find local businesses.
   You're in [X] of them. Here's the full list and where you stand."

WHAT WE DO ABOUT IT (the Fibonacco explanation — specific, not abstract):
  "[Community Name] Day.News is a local news publication covering [Community].
   We publish daily. We cover local business, events, and community news.
   [X] residents read us regularly. Here's how your business shows up:"

  Then the concrete list — not features, but presence:
  - "Day.News — your business mentioned in local news coverage [X] times/month"
  - "GoEventCity — your events promoted to [Y] local event seekers"
  - "Downtown Guide — your business listed and ranked in the [Category] directory"
  - "AlphaSite — your professional web presence at [business].alphasite.com"
  - "Community newsletters — your business featured to [Z] email subscribers"

YOUR SITUATION (personalized if possible):
  "Right now, your business [is/is not] listed in [Community]'s directory."
  "Your category ([Category]) has [X] of [Y] premium slots filled."
  "Here's how you compare to other [Category] businesses in the network:"

THE AM NOTE (project manager voice):
  "I'm [AM Name], your account manager for [Community Name].
   I've been looking at the [Category] positions in your community and
   I want to make sure you're aware of what's available.
   If you want to discuss your options, I'm here: [contact].
   If you want to see your proposal, it's ready: [link]."

DUAL CTA:
  PRIMARY: "[See My Proposal]" / "[Check My Community's Status]"
  SECONDARY: "[Learn How Events Work]" / "[See a Sample Business Listing]"
    → Links to relevant Hook or How-To

SLOT STATUS: Always present. Always real numbers.
```

## TYPE 3: HOW-TO

### Framework: Instructional — do the thing, then see what's next

**What it is:** A step-by-step guide that gets them through a specific task. Dashboard setup. Event posting. Email marketing configuration. They're here to execute, not to learn.

**The owner's mindset:** "I have 10 minutes. Tell me what to click. Let me see if this works. Then I'll decide if I want more."

### Page Structure

```
HEADLINE: Task-oriented, with time estimate
  "Set Up Your Business Listing — 5 Minutes"
  "Post Your First Event — 3 Steps"
  "Connect Your Email Marketing — 10 Minutes"

WHAT YOU'LL HAVE WHEN YOU'RE DONE (1-2 sentences):
  "When you finish, your business listing will be live on Day.News,
   Downtown Guide, and GoEventCity. Local residents will be able
   to find you starting today."

PREREQUISITES (if any — keep to one line):
  "You'll need: your business address, phone number, and a logo."

STEPS (4-6 max):
  Each step:
  ├── Step number + verb-first title: "1. Log into your dashboard"
  ├── What to do: 1-2 sentences with specific clicks/fields
  ├── Screenshot: What you should see
  └── Done indicator: "You'll see a green checkmark when this is saved"

  No "why" explanations in the steps. No context. No theory.
  If they need to understand WHY, that's an Education page — link to it.

VERIFICATION:
  "Check your work: visit [URL] and confirm your listing appears."
  "Expected: Your listing is live within [X] hours."

WHAT'S NEXT (the 4Ps nudge — 1 section, not a pitch):
  "Now that your listing is live, here are two things that will
   make it work harder for you:"
  - "[Add a coupon]" → Hook page link
  - "[Post your next event]" → Hook page link
  - "[See how your listing is performing]" → Dashboard link

  AM note (1 sentence):
  "If you want help with any of this, I'm here: [AM contact]."

SLOT STATUS: Present but minimal. These are utility pages.
```

## TYPE 4: CONVERSION

### Framework: AM Project Management — 3 meetings, not 5

**What it is:** The AM walks the prospect through the process of securing their position in the community. Not a sales funnel. A project with a timeline, deliverables, and a decision point.

**The project:** "Get [Business Name] positioned in [Community Name] before their [Category] slots fill."

**Why 3 meetings, not 5:**
- Meeting 1: "Here's what we are and here's your situation" (Briefing + Status)
- Meeting 2: "Here's your proposal — everything you get and what it costs" (Proposal)
- Meeting 3: "Decision time — your slot status has changed" (Close)

An SMB owner doesn't need 5 meetings to make a $300/month decision. They need to understand what it is (Meeting 1), see the deal (Meeting 2), and decide (Meeting 3). If they need more meetings than that, the product isn't clear enough or the timing isn't right — and more meetings won't fix either problem.

**The takeaway sale at every meeting:** The AM isn't pressuring. The AM is flagging a real project risk: "I'm concerned that if we don't move on this, your [Category] slot will fill and I won't be able to get you in. I'm not trying to rush you — I'm trying to manage the timeline."

### CONV-003: The Close

**Purpose:** Decision time. Trial ending, or sufficient time has passed since the proposal. The AM is flagging a project risk: the window is closing.

**AM voice:** "I wanted to give you a status update. Your situation has changed and I want to make sure you're aware before I can't do anything about it."

**Time to consume:** 2 minutes

**Personalization: Tier 3 (business-specific)**

```
SECTION 1: STATUS UPDATE

  Headline: "[Business Name] — Status Update from [AM Name]"

  "I'm writing because your situation in [Community Name] has changed
   since we last spoke. Here's where things stand:"

  STATUS BLOCK (live data, factual, no drama):
  ├── Your category: [Category]
  ├── Slots when I first contacted you: [original count]
  ├── Slots remaining today: [current count]
  ├── Change: [X] positions filled since [date]
  ├── Founder pricing: [days remaining] OR "expired on [date]"
  └── Your proposal: still active at $300/month

  If trial is ending:
  ├── "Your trial ends on [date]"
  ├── "What you'll keep: basic listing (not premium)"
  ├── "What you'll lose: priority placement, news mentions,
  │    featured events, newsletter placement, coupons, AlphaSite"
  └── "Your trial results: [X] views, [Y] mentions, [Z] reader reach"

SECTION 2: THE AM'S RECOMMENDATION

  "Here's my honest assessment:

   [If slots are genuinely scarce:]
   I think you should lock this in. [Category] started with [X] slots
   and [Y] are gone. At this rate, the remaining [Z] slots will fill
   within [timeframe]. Once they're full, the next option is Community
   Sponsor at a higher rate, or the waitlist.

   [If founder pricing is expiring:]
   The Founder rate of $300/month locked for 3 years expires on [date].
   After that, new members pay the standard rate. If you're going to
   join, doing it before [date] saves you real money.

   [If trial data is strong:]
   In your trial, you've reached [X] local readers, been mentioned
   [Y] times, and your listing has been viewed [Z] times. That stops
   on [trial end date] unless you activate your membership.

   I'm not trying to pressure you. I'm telling you what I see in the
   data and I want you to make an informed decision."

SECTION 3: THE ASK (single CTA)

  "[Activate My Membership — $300/month]" ← checkout
  "[I Have Questions — Call Me]" ← AM phone/calendar

  No "save for later." No "tell me more." They've had the briefing
  and the proposal. The options are: yes, talk, or not now.

  If not now:
  "If the timing isn't right, I respect that. I'll keep your proposal
```
