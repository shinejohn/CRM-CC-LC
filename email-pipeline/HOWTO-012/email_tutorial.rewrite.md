# HOWTO-012/email_tutorial — Rewrite
SUBJECT: When a bad review comes in
PREVIEW: It's not the review that decides — it's what you do next.
BODY:
Hi [First Name],

A one-star lands. Your stomach drops. You want to fire back, or pretend you never saw it.

Here's what most owners miss: the people reading that review aren't really judging the complaint. They're watching what you do next. A calm, human reply — owning it, offering to make it right — tells the whole town more about you than five stars ever could. Handled well, the person who left it often comes back. Some even change the star.

Your name in [Community Name] gets built one reply at a time. The good reviews are the easy part: thank them by name, mention the thing they loved, tell them to come back soon.

The hard ones — the angry, the unfair, the 1am rant — have a playbook of their own, and it's worth getting right before the next one shows up.

[AM Name]

*P.S. — One rule covers almost everything: reply within a day, every time. A fast, kind answer is the most disarming thing on the internet. What's the toughest review you've ever gotten? Reply and I'll tell you exactly how I'd answer it.*
