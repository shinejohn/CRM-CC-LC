The customers who stop coming back almost never tell you why. They don't complain. They just quietly don't return — and you never find out it was the long wait, or the parking, or one small thing you could've fixed in an afternoon.

The owners who ask get to hear it. Not a big production — just a short, honest "how'd we do, and what would you change?" a day or two after someone visits [Business Name]. A couple of replies is all it takes to spot the thing that's quietly costing you repeat business.

Picture hearing that the wait feels long — and fixing it with a coffee station and a few magazines, so people start booking their next visit on the way out the door.

Ask your folks what they actually think. It's the cheapest improvement you'll ever make.
