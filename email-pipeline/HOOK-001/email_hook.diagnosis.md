# HOOK-001 / email_hook — Diagnosis
SCORE: 3/5
THIN: no
Ask (single? what is it): Claim your free directory listing. Effectively one ask, but split across two buttons ([SEE YOUR LISTING] and [CLAIM YOUR LISTING]) which dilutes it.
Lesson (present? state it): "You're already listed — claiming just gives you control of what's there." Works on two levels: useful (verify your public info) + nudge (get inside Day.News).
Story/angle: A-to-B — predictable "here's your listing, here's the button," but the "we already built it from public info" angle earns some interest.
Opening: concrete — "Your business is already listed in [Community Name]'s business directory." Drops into a specific claim, not throat-clearing. Good.
Subject verdict: ~61 chars; describe (not a loop); no hard spam triggers ("free" is in preview not subject); reads human but plain.
Preview verdict: repeats — "Claim it free in 5 minutes" restates the ask instead of deepening it; wastes the second-job slot and front-loads "free."
CTA count: 2 (flag — SEE + CLAIM compete; should be one path)
Hold-back / click-driver: ok — "Here's what it looks like" creates a reason to click through to see the listing.
Hero: reader — focuses on their business and their info, though benefit list is feature-shaped.
Voice: human
Top 3 problems to fix: 1) Collapse to ONE CTA — lead with "see your listing" as the click, fold "claim" into it. 2) Rewrite preview to do a second job (e.g., finish a thought / add the surprise that it's already built), not repeat "claim it free." 3) Tighten the four-arrow benefit list — it reads as a feature dump; lead with the most human benefit ("see who's viewing your listing").
One-line overall: Solid concrete opener and a genuine "already built for you" angle, dragged to average by a repeating preview and two competing CTAs.
