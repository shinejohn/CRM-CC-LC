# Exclusive Inventory — "Own Your Corner" (the honest scarcity lever)

> The strongest honest scarcity we have. Not a fake countdown — **real, finite, category-limited inventory.**
> Some positions are one-of-a-kind in a town. And it's deeply on-mission: a community that gets its **wisdom and
> trust from the specific people next door** — its own doctor, its own attorney, the funeral home families trust.
> Pairs with `AUDIENCE.md` / `VISION.md`. Use this in every paid ask and in the close.

## What's genuinely scarce (grounded in the real product)

| The exclusive | What it is | Real product |
|---|---|---|
| **The columns** | One trusted local voice per topic — the **health column** by a local doctor, the **law column** by a local attorney, the money column by a local CPA. Naturally *one per topic*, because trust is personal. | Community Expert **+ $100 column** |
| **The sections** | A section of the paper stands behind one business — the **obituary section** belongs to one funeral home; the high-school sports section to one sponsor. | Community Sponsor **+ section fee** |
| **Category search / featured** | When a neighbor searches "[plumber / dentist / roofer]," only a few names show first. Limited spots, on purpose. | Featured / category slots |
| **The town's capacity** | ~**37 marquee slots per community**, with per-category limits. When a category's spots fill, they're gone until one opens. | The slot model |

## Why it works (and stays honest)
- **It's true.** The caps are real; the columns and sections are genuinely exclusive. No manufactured urgency — and a been-burned owner can *feel* the difference between real scarcity and a fake clock.
- **It flips the two hardest objections.** **Fit** — "this is for someone like me, the [Category] in *my* town." **Competitor loss** — "if I don't take it, the shop down the street will, and then *they're* the name neighbors learn first."
- **It's the mission, made tangible.** A town whose health advice comes from its own doctor and whose obituaries are held by a funeral home families trust — that's neighbors trusting neighbors, not a search box three time zones away.

## How to use it (the moves)
1. **Pride + identity:** sell the *position*, not a listing — "Be THE [Category] in [Community Name]."
2. **Honest scarcity:** "There's one health column / one obituary section, and only a handful of [Category] spots. When they're taken, they're taken."
3. **Gentle, true competitor stakes:** "If it isn't you, it'll be someone down the street."
4. **Tie to the fair model:** still the simple, shared-cost deal from Email 8 — the column is the Expert membership + a small column fee; the section is the Sponsor membership + a section fee. Lead with value/authority/community; land the scarcity; tuck the price in the P.S.

## Where it lives in the journey
The **earned-ask stretch** (after the 3 weeks of trust) and — above all — the **close (CONV)**. Real exclusivity is the only honest reason to act *now*, which is exactly what a conversion email needs.

## A caution on numbers (don't fabricate)
The marquee exclusives are naturally singular (one obituary section, one health column). For *counts* (how many plumber spots, how many left), use a token `[X spots left in [Community Name]]` filled from **live inventory data** — never an invented number. If a category's exact cap isn't known at send time, say "a handful" / "only so many," which is true, rather than a fake figure.
