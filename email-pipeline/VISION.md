# The Vision & The Trust-First Opening

> Pairs with `AUDIENCE.md`. AUDIENCE = who we're writing to. VISION = why we exist and how we earn their trust
> before we ever ask for anything. Every opening email is judged against this.

## The mission (the thing we're actually building)

**A community that gets its fun, its suppliers, its wisdom, and its help from the people who live next door.**

- Its **fun** — where's the live music, the trivia night, the festival, the thing to do this weekend → from neighbors.
- Its **suppliers** — who fixes the sink, caters the party, does the taxes, cuts the hair → the shop down the street.
- Its **wisdom** — who knows this town, who's an expert, who to trust → the people who live here.
- Its **help** — when someone needs a hand, a recommendation, a hire → the network of neighbors.

Day.News isn't a marketing tool. It's the **town square, rebuilt** — the place a community turns to find each other.
The businesses, nonprofits, and civic leaders we email are not "leads." They are the **pillars that make a town a
town.** We're inviting them to help build the place they already love — and benefit because they helped build it.

## The relationship thesis (give-to-get, applied to the sequence)

The Million Dollar Emails rule: **be the name they're glad to see.** You earn the ask by giving first. The current
campaign breaks this — it asks on day 1 ("claim your listing"), day 5 ("post your event"), day 7 ("claim again").
That's the taker pattern. We're flipping it.

**The new opening doesn't ask them to do anything for us. It does something for them, and for the town, first.**

What we already have that lets us give before we ask (and it's genuine, not a trick):
- We **already built their listing** from public info — they're already part of the town's new front page.
- We **already publish their community's news** — we can put them, and the town, in a good light for free.
- We can **recognize** them — as a local pillar, a neighbor's recommendation, part of what makes the town good.

## The opening arc — earn attention, then trust, then the (easy) first yes

| Touch | Job | What we GIVE | The ask (if any) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Belonging** | "Your town has its own paper now — and you're already in it." Make them feel seen as part of the community fabric. Tell them the vision. | None but "take a look" / "just reply." |
| 2 | **Recognition / usefulness** | Show them something real — their place in the town's news, or a genuinely useful local thing. Give a coin. | Soft. No pressure. |
| 3 | **The easy first yes** | Now — only now — a tiny, free, no-risk ask: "make sure your neighbors see you right." Framed as *for them and the town*, not "claim your listing." | One easy yes. |
| 4+ | Keep giving (the existing arc, re-aimed) | Useful local things, recognition, stories — three or four gives for every ask. | Earned, spaced. |

Rule of thumb from the framework: **3–4 genuine gives for every ask.** The current opening is ~all asks. Fix that.

## Voice for the opening (tone north-star)

Write like **the person who started the town paper because they love the place** — warm, plain, a little proud,
talking to a neighbor they respect. Short sentences. A real scene. No jargon (`AUDIENCE.md` banned list). The
reader should finish the first email feeling *seen and part of something*, not *sold to*.
